# S2-C-R2 Research Guide

## Title

Plant Resilience And Recovery Profiles

## Research home

- Sphere: `S2`
- Lane: `S2-C Stress Biology & Resilience`
- Research line: `S2-C-R2`

## Research question

Which recovery-linked signatures are strong enough to distinguish plants that merely survive stress from plants that actually recover function, structure, and adaptive capacity after stress relief?

## Why this line matters

`S2-C-R1` covers stress-response signatures.

`S2-C-R2` must go one step further.

It should identify:

- recovery-linked signals
- rewatering trajectories
- resilience markers that stay informative after the stress window

Without this line, `S2-C` remains mostly a stress-detection lane.

## Scope

The first execution pass should stay narrow:

- drought and rewatering or drought-release contexts
- recovery-phase physiology and molecular responses
- genotype-aware resilience interpretation where possible

## Proposed substudies

### `S2-C-R2a` Resilience Marker Prioritization

Focus on:

- hydraulic recovery
- chlorophyll and photosynthesis recovery
- hormone-signaling reset or persistence
- immune activation after rewatering

### `S2-C-R2b` Recovery-Phase Signature Analysis

Focus on:

- recovery trajectories across time
- genotype-specific recovery differences
- which traits recover early, late, or incompletely

## Main outputs

- recovery-signature evidence table
- resilience marker shortlist
- one recovery-layer figure
- one stress-vs-recovery comparison note

## What this is not

This is not:

- a yield optimization guide
- an irrigation prescription
- a crop management playbook

Those belong later outside `S2`.

## Immediate next step

Build the first compact recovery panel with:

- 5-7 recovery-linked records
- at least one molecular rewatering study
- at least one physiology-first recovery study
