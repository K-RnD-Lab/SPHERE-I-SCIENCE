## Interpretation

### Recovery is its own state

The strongest conceptual result is that recovery should not be treated as a simple reversal of drought.

At least one high-quality study shows that rehydration produces a distinct immune-active state rather than a passive reset.

### Recovery quality is duration-dependent

Physiology-first studies show that some traits recover quickly, while others remain partially impaired depending on stress duration.

That means resilience is not only "can the plant survive", but also "how completely and how fast can key functions return".

### Genotype matters

Sorghum and tomato evidence both support the same practical lesson:

- recovery capacity varies across genotypes
- not all rewatered plants return to the same functional state

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- recovery-phase signatures are real and interpretable
- resilience can be tracked through physiological and molecular layers together
- incomplete recovery is as informative as strong recovery

At this stage we should not say:

- that one universal recovery index fits every plant system
- that one marker predicts resilience across all crops
- that post-drought recovery automatically translates to yield recovery
