# S2-B-R1 Preliminary Findings

## Research home

- Sphere: `S2`
- Lane: `S2-B Phytochemicals & Natural Products`
- Research line: `S2-B-R1 Phytochemical Atlas And Candidate Prioritization`

## Scope v0

This first active execution pass narrows `S2-B-R1` to:

- edible and medicinal plant-derived compounds
- oxidative-stress and inflammation modulation
- atlas-first ranking, not therapeutic claiming

That scope is intentionally tighter than the original lane description.

Without a narrow theme, `S2-B-R1` would quickly collapse into a generic list of "interesting plant compounds" with weak research value.

## Current shortlist

### Tier 1

- `Curcumin` from `Curcuma longa`
- `EGCG` from `Camellia sinensis`
- `Resveratrol` from `Vitis vinifera`

### Tier 2

- `Quercetin` from `Allium cepa`
- `Genistein` from `Glycine max`
- `Xanthohumol` from `Humulus lupulus`

## Why these six

They were selected because they jointly provide:

- clear plant-source identity
- recognizable compound classes
- repeated review-backed bioactivity discussion
- mechanism notes that can be compared across compounds
- enough diversity to avoid an atlas made only of one plant family

## Practical interpretation

The current shortlist already supports three real outputs:

1. a small normalized evidence table
2. a ranked candidate panel
3. a first compound-class comparison figure

## Candidate logic

### Curcumin

- strongest benchmark for pathway-rich anti-inflammatory plant polyphenol logic
- useful as a reference candidate, not because it is novel, but because it calibrates the atlas

### EGCG

- unusually clear plant-compound relationship
- useful as a "source certainty" benchmark

### Resveratrol

- strong bridge between plant chemistry and stress-adaptation signaling
- good comparator for fruit-derived polyphenol logic

### Quercetin

- excellent food-linked flavonol reference
- broad literature means it must be constrained carefully by source plant and context

### Genistein

- important soybean/isoflavone line
- keeps `S2-B` from drifting into only tea, spice, and fruit compounds

### Xanthohumol

- the least generic candidate in the current set
- important because it opens a hop/prenylflavonoid sub-branch rather than repeating mainstream polyphenol stories

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- these compounds have strong enough literature presence to justify atlas inclusion
- they show repeatable oxidative-stress and inflammation-related pathway relevance
- they are valid candidates for a plant-first prioritization layer

At this stage we should not say:

- that any of them is clinically validated by this project
- that this is a therapeutic recommendation
- that cross-study comparability is already solved

## Immediate next step

The next execution move for `S2-B-R1` should be:

- add 4-6 more records only if they extend diversity
- not if they merely repeat the same polyphenol story

The best next additions are likely:

- one glucosinolate-linked candidate
- one rosmarinic-acid or caffeic-acid lineage candidate
- one carotenoid or terpenoid candidate

That will prevent the atlas from becoming a narrow "famous polyphenols" list.
