# S2-E-R2 Research Guide

## Title

Comparative Translational Profiles For Plant-Derived Candidates

## Research home

- Sphere: `S2`
- Lane: `S2-E Translational Plant Bioactivity`
- Research line: `S2-E-R2`

## Research question

How can plant-derived candidate molecules be compared transparently so that translational opportunity is not confused with translational readiness?

## Why this line matters

`S2-E-R1` gives `S2` a translational shortlist.

`S2-E-R2` must do the harder part:

- compare candidate families
- make limitations explicit
- separate biological promise from delivery and standardization friction

Without this line, `S2-E` risks becoming a list of interesting compounds with no disciplined comparison logic.

## Scope

The first execution pass should stay narrow:

- use the current `S2-B-R1` shortlist as the starting pool
- compare compounds, not disease areas
- focus on source clarity, mechanism breadth, evidence maturity, and translational friction

## Proposed substudies

### `S2-E-R2a` Candidate Family Comparison

Focus on:

- polyphenol vs isoflavone vs prenylflavonoid logic
- which candidate classes are easier to translate structurally
- where source-to-compound identity is strongest

### `S2-E-R2b` Translational Plausibility Scoring

Focus on:

- bioavailability friction
- standardization risk
- safety or dosing caveats
- formulation dependence

## Main outputs

- comparative translational evidence table
- ranked opportunity vs friction panel
- one shortlist figure
- one summary note on which candidates are strong, weak, or premature

## What this is not

This is not:

- a clinical recommendation layer
- a therapeutic ranking for patients
- a product development protocol

It is a comparison framework for deciding what deserves deeper research.

## Immediate next step

Build the first comparative panel with:

- 5-6 candidate records
- one opportunity score
- one translational friction score
- one interpretation note per candidate
