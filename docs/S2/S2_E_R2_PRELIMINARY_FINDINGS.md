## Scope v0

This first active execution pass narrows `S2-E-R2` to:

- six benchmark candidates already anchored in `S2-B-R1`
- comparison by opportunity and translational friction
- no disease-specific therapeutic claims

This keeps the work disciplined and stops `S2-E` from drifting into marketing-style shortlist language.

## Current comparative panel

### Strong but friction-heavy

- curcumin
- resveratrol
- EGCG

### Balanced comparators

- quercetin
- genistein

### Promising but higher-risk branch

- xanthohumol

## Interpretation

### Curcumin

- still one of the strongest benchmark candidates for mechanism breadth
- but also one of the clearest examples of why translational promise and translational readiness are not the same

### EGCG

- strongest source clarity in the current panel
- broad mechanism relevance
- but safety and dose concerns prevent treating it as friction-free

### Resveratrol

- one of the best conceptual translational candidates
- but repeated bioavailability problems keep it from becoming a straightforward lead

### Quercetin

- less singular than curcumin or EGCG
- but comparatively useful because it is broadly supported and not as tied to one narrow source context

### Genistein

- important because it brings chemical diversity beyond the usual polyphenol story
- good comparator for how a soybean-linked isoflavone differs from canonical spice/tea/fruit candidates

### Xanthohumol

- strongest "interesting but not yet easy" case
- useful because it prevents the panel from rewarding only mainstream compounds

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- some candidates are biologically compelling but translationally high-friction
- source clarity and mechanism breadth do not always move together
- the best early translational candidates are not automatically the most famous compounds

At this stage we should not say:

- that the top-ranked compound is product-ready
- that low-friction means clinically validated
- that this panel replaces disease-specific evidence review

## Immediate next step

The next execution move for `S2-E-R2` should be:

- add one non-polyphenol comparator if possible
- add one formulation-focused evidence layer
- then convert the panel into an opportunity-vs-friction figure
