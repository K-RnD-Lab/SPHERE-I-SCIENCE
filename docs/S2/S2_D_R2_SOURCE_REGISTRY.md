# S2-D-R2 Source Registry

## Research home

- Sphere: `S2`
- Lane: `S2-D Plant-Pathogen & Defense Systems`
- Research line: `S2-D-R2 Host-Pathogen Interaction Interpretation`

## Purpose

This registry tracks the sources used to compare resistant and susceptible host states across plant-pathogen systems.

The goal is not to build a generic disease list.

The goal is to capture repeated host-side patterns that distinguish:

- resistant from susceptible states
- faster from delayed immune activation
- coordinated defense from dysregulated collapse

## Current active scope

For the first execution pass, `S2-D-R2` is narrowed to:

- resistant vs susceptible host comparisons
- bacterial and fungal disease systems
- transcriptomic or transcriptome-metabolome studies

## Current stance

At this stage, `S2-D-R2` should be treated as:

- a host-comparison interpretation layer

not as:

- a universal resistance ontology
- a direct breeding prescription

## What to collect next

The next useful sources should include:

- one additional fungal temporal comparison
- one clearer susceptibility-linked auxin or metabolic-collapse paper
- one study with explicit resistant vs susceptible metabolite contrast

## Claim discipline

From this registry, we may claim:

- that resistant and susceptible host states are often separated by timing, coordination, and pathway balance
- that recurrent host-side patterns can be extracted across systems
- that resistance is often structured rather than simply stronger

We should not yet claim:

- universal quantitative thresholds
- crop-independent predictor panels
- direct field translation
