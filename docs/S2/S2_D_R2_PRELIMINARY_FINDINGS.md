## Current recurring host patterns

### Strong resistance-associated patterns

- early and large-scale transcriptional mobilization
- stronger PRR / MAPK / WRKY or TF-network activation
- phenylpropanoid, phytoalexin, or defense-metabolite reinforcement
- structured metabolic reallocation instead of chaotic collapse

### Strong susceptibility-associated patterns

- delayed ROS detoxification
- weak or failed immune activation
- dysregulated signaling
- metabolic collapse or misrouted hormone balance

## Interpretation

### Resistance is often coordinated, not merely stronger

The clearest repeated lesson is that resistant hosts are not simply "more activated".

They are more coordinated:

- earlier
- more layered
- more metabolically organized

### Timing matters

Temporal studies show that resistant hosts often establish a phased response:

- early containment
- mid-stage immune escalation
- later metabolic or physiological stabilization

Susceptible hosts often fail on timing before they fail on magnitude.

### Vulnerability has structure too

Susceptibility should not be treated as a blank or passive state.

The current evidence suggests recognizable failure patterns:

- delayed detox
- low DEG mobilization
- auxin or growth-linked misbalance
- incoherent signal transduction

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- recurrent resistant-vs-susceptible patterns can be extracted across plant systems
- resistance often behaves as a phased, coordinated state
- susceptibility often behaves as a delayed or dysregulated state

At this stage we should not say:

- that all resistant systems share one universal signature
- that one pathosystem automatically predicts another
- that this comparative layer is ready for direct field use
