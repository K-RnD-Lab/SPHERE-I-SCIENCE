# S2-A To S2-E Research Map

## Purpose

This document turns `S2-A ... S2-E` into a practical lane-level map.

The goal is to keep:

- the lane stable
- two main research directions inside each lane
- one to two substudies inside each research direction

`S2` should stay plant-intrinsic and phytochemistry-first, not drift into agricultural intervention systems.

## Structural rule

Use:

- `S2-A`, `S2-B`, `S2-C`, `S2-D`, `S2-E` as stable lanes
- `R1`, `R2` as the two main directions inside each lane
- `R1a`, `R1b`, `R2a`, `R2b` as substudies when needed

## S2-A Plant Omics & Trait Signatures

### Role

Plant omics, molecular traits, and interpretable plant-state signatures.

### `S2-A-R1` Trait Signature Discovery

#### Substudies

- `S2-A-R1a` genotype-to-trait signal mapping
- `S2-A-R1b` organ- or tissue-specific molecular signatures

#### Why this stays central

This gives `S2` a stable omics backbone that can support later phytochemical and resilience work.

### `S2-A-R2` Plant State Classification

#### Substudies

- `S2-A-R2a` healthy vs stressed state classification
- `S2-A-R2b` developmental or condition-specific signature panels

#### Why this matters

This turns omics from descriptive data into interpretable state logic.

## S2-B Phytochemicals & Natural Products

### Role

Plant-derived compounds, bioactive molecule organization, and natural-product prioritization.

### `S2-B-R1` Phytochemical Atlas And Candidate Prioritization

#### Substudies

- `S2-B-R1a` species-to-compound registry
- `S2-B-R1b` compound class and bioactivity ranking

#### Why this stays central

This is the clearest and most durable translational entry point for `S2`.

### `S2-B-R2` Plant Compound Target And Mechanism Logic

#### Substudies

- `S2-B-R2a` target-linked candidate compounds
- `S2-B-R2b` pathway-aware natural product scoring

#### Why this matters

This prevents `S2-B` from staying only a catalog and pushes it toward actionable interpretation.

## S2-C Stress Biology & Resilience

### Role

Plant stress response, resilience mechanisms, and adaptation signatures.

### `S2-C-R1` Stress Response Signatures

#### Substudies

- `S2-C-R1a` abiotic stress signature panels
- `S2-C-R1b` biotic stress response markers

#### Why this stays central

This is one of the strongest long-term biological lines in `S2`.

### `S2-C-R2` Resilience And Recovery Profiles

#### Substudies

- `S2-C-R2a` resilience marker prioritization
- `S2-C-R2b` recovery-phase signature analysis

#### Why this matters

This gives `S2-C` more depth than only stress detection and opens a durable direction for future translational use.

## S2-D Plant-Pathogen & Defense Systems

### Role

Defense biology, pathogen response, and plant protection logic from the plant side.

### `S2-D-R1` Plant Defense Signatures

#### Substudies

- `S2-D-R1a` immune-response molecular patterns
- `S2-D-R1b` defense metabolite and pathway signals

#### Why this stays central

This gives `S2-D` a clean biological identity without pulling it into applied field intervention too early.

### `S2-D-R2` Host-Pathogen Interaction Interpretation

#### Substudies

- `S2-D-R2a` pathogen-response comparison profiles
- `S2-D-R2b` host vulnerability and resistance markers

#### Why this matters

This creates a useful bridge between plant molecular biology and future agricultural translation.

## S2-E Translational Plant Bioactivity

### Role

The translational layer that links plant compounds and plant biology to possible functional applications.

### `S2-E-R1` Bioactivity Candidate Translation

#### Substudies

- `S2-E-R1a` plant-derived bioactivity candidate shortlist
- `S2-E-R1b` mechanism-linked plant molecule prioritization

#### Why this stays central

This is where `S2` can connect to practical human-facing or biotech-facing relevance without leaving plant science.

### `S2-E-R2` Comparative Translational Profiles

#### Substudies

- `S2-E-R2a` compare plant-derived candidate families
- `S2-E-R2b` evidence scoring for translational plausibility

#### Why this matters

This gives `S2-E` a durable way to evaluate candidates rather than just listing them.

## Operational interpretation

### What is already useful now

Even before deep repo expansion, `S2` can become useful as:

- a phytochemical knowledge system
- a plant stress signature system
- a plant-to-bioactivity interpretation layer

### What should not happen

Do not let `S2` drift into:

- field deployment
- yield-first intervention logic
- agricultural operational systems

Those belong in `S3`.

## Recommended order inside S2

Best order of execution:

1. `S2-B`
2. `S2-C`
3. `S2-A`
4. `S2-E`
5. `S2-D`

Reason:

- `S2-B` and `S2-C` are the strongest starting lines
- `S2-A` gives omics backbone after that
- `S2-E` becomes stronger once compounds and stress signals are defined
- `S2-D` benefits from having the rest of the plant system clearer first
