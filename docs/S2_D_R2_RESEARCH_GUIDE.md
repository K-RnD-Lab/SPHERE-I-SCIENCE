# S2-D-R2 Research Guide

## Title

Host-Pathogen Interaction Interpretation Across Resistant And Susceptible Plant States

## Research home

- Sphere: `S2`
- Lane: `S2-D Plant-Pathogen & Defense Systems`
- Research line: `S2-D-R2`

## Research question

When resistant and susceptible plant states are compared directly, which recurring host-side patterns are strong enough to support a cross-system interpretation layer?

## Why this line matters

`S2-D-R1` gave `S2-D` a compact defense-signature panel.

`S2-D-R2` should answer a different question:

- what repeatedly distinguishes resistant and susceptible host states
- which patterns are robust across different plant-pathogen systems
- where resistance means fast, coordinated reprogramming rather than just "more defense"

Without this line, `S2-D` stays at the marker level but does not gain comparative interpretive logic.

## Scope

The first execution pass should stay narrow:

- resistant vs susceptible or tolerant vs susceptible host comparisons
- bacterial and fungal disease contexts
- host-side transcriptome/metabolome interpretation

## Proposed substudies

### `S2-D-R2a` Pathogen-Response Comparison Profiles

Focus on:

- resistant vs susceptible transcriptional amplitude
- early vs delayed response timing
- pathway-level reprogramming differences

### `S2-D-R2b` Host Vulnerability And Resistance Markers

Focus on:

- phenylpropanoid and secondary-metabolite reinforcement
- PRR/MAPK/WRKY and TF-network differences
- susceptibility-linked auxin or metabolic collapse signals

## Main outputs

- comparative evidence table
- resistant-vs-susceptible interpretation panel
- one summary figure of recurring host patterns
- one short report on robust comparative signals

## What this is not

This is not:

- a pathogen taxonomy document
- a crop management protocol
- a universal resistance rulebook

It is a comparative interpretation layer for host-side plant defense logic.

## Immediate next step

Build the first comparative panel with:

- 5-7 host comparison records
- at least one bacterial and two fungal contexts
- explicit notes on what repeats and what is system-specific
