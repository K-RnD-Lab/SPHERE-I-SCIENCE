# S3-B-R2 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Biofertilizer Reliability, Quality Control, And Failure Modes: A Practical Evidence Review

## Research home

- Sphere: `S3`
- Lane: `S3-B Biofertilizer Design & Validation`
- Research line: `S3-B-R2 Biofertilizer Reliability, Quality Control, And Failure Modes`

## Research question

How can microbial biofertilizer systems be screened for reliability and deployment readiness beyond isolated efficacy claims?

## Scope

This report covers:

- product reliability signals
- formulation and shelf-life issues
- labeling and identity integrity
- field inconsistency and deployment failure modes

This report does not attempt:

- a full market audit
- a regulatory comparison by country
- a universal ranking of all inoculant classes

## Data included

- reviews on field inconsistency
- product-assessment papers
- quality-framework papers
- formulation studies relevant to reliability

## Methods

1. collect source-backed evidence on reliability bottlenecks
2. normalize recurring failure modes
3. separate direct product-testing evidence from general reviews
4. identify mitigation signals that are practical rather than speculative

## Outputs

- one reliability evidence table
- one failure-mode matrix
- one minimum quality-check checklist
- one compact interpretation note

## Figures

Possible figures:

1. failure-mode map
2. reliability checklist diagram
3. product-quality vs deployment-readiness matrix

## Limits

- evidence comes from mixed inoculant classes
- some high-level reviews synthesize rather than test products directly
- strong product-audit evidence is still relatively scarce

## Conclusion

The report should conclude with:

- what makes a microbial product more trustworthy
- what repeatedly breaks deployment reliability
- what minimum evidence should be demanded before field-facing use
