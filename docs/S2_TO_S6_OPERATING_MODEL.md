# S2 To S6 Operating Model

## Goal

`S2-S6` should use the same stable logic as `S1`:

- sphere = long-term domain home
- sub-lane = repeatable theme inside the sphere
- research item = concrete study or case

This avoids mixing major scientific domains with one-off project names.

## General rule

For `S2-S6`, do not create many folders immediately.

First fix the stable sub-lane map in documentation.
Then create `R1`, `R2`, and later case-study folders only when a direction becomes real work.

That means:

- keep the sphere
- define the lettered sub-lanes
- activate only the first 1-2 sub-lanes with real research
- leave the rest as roadmap, not empty folder clutter

## S2 Plant Science & Phytochemistry

### Stable sub-lanes

- `S2-A` Plant Omics & Trait Signatures
- `S2-B` Phytochemicals & Natural Products
- `S2-C` Stress Biology & Resilience
- `S2-D` Plant-Pathogen & Defense Systems
- `S2-E` Translational Plant Bioactivity

### Best first priorities

- `S2-B`
- `S2-C`

### Why

These are the clearest starting lines if the aim is practical value for researchers and future translational use.

## S6 Ecology & Environmental Science

### Stable sub-lanes

- `S6-A` Environmental Sentinel Systems
- `S6-B` Environmental Microbiomes
- `S6-C` Biodiversity & Ecosystem Signals
- `S6-D` Pollution, Stress & Response Networks
- `S6-E` Climate-Linked Ecological Change

### Best first priorities

- `S6-A`
- `S6-D`

### Why

These are the most operational and socially legible directions: they can support both scientific cases and public-impact narratives.

`S6-A` should be the natural home of `K-EcoLOGIC Lab`, because that work is not primarily about environmental microbiomes. It is about environmental monitoring, decision support, risk communication, sorting logic, and public-interest environmental intelligence.

## Activation rule

Only create subfolders when a direction becomes active.

Example:

- `S2-B-R1`
- `S3-A-R1`
- `S4-A-R1`

Do not prebuild dozens of empty directories just because the map exists.

## Archive rule

If future work appears as isolated one-off experiments, do not let it redefine the sphere.

Instead:

- keep the sphere stable
- place the work under the correct sub-lane
- archive it later if it does not become a repeated research line

## Recommended order after S1

1. `S2`
2. `S3`
3. `S5`
4. `S4`
5. `S6`

Reason:

- `S2` and `S3` are easier to operationalize next
- `S5` is strategically strong and human-relevant
- `S4` is cross-cutting and becomes stronger once neighboring spheres are defined
- `S6` benefits from the same discipline but can be slightly later
