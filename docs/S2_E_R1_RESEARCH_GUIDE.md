# S2-E-R1 Research Guide

## Research home

- Sphere: `S2`
- Lane: `S2-E Translational Plant Bioactivity`
- Research line: `S2-E-R1 Bioactivity Candidate Translation`

## Why this matters

`S2-E-R1` is the first clear translational bridge inside `S2`.

This is where the sphere stops being only:

- plant omics
- compound cataloging
- stress-signature interpretation

and begins to ask:

- which candidates are worth translating further
- which plant-derived signals have the strongest biological plausibility
- which findings can be organized into a structured translational shortlist

## Core question

How can plant-derived molecular findings be converted into a transparent shortlist of bioactivity candidates that remains evidence-aware and useful for downstream translational work?

## Practical substudies

### `S2-E-R1a` Plant-Derived Bioactivity Candidate Shortlist

Goal:

- build a ranked shortlist of plant-derived candidates with plausible biological relevance

Focus:

- candidate selection
- evidence strength
- shortlist transparency

### `S2-E-R1b` Mechanism-Linked Plant Molecule Prioritization

Goal:

- prioritize plant molecules by the strength of their mechanistic rationale

Focus:

- mechanism context
- candidate plausibility
- translational ranking

## Why this matters now

Recent literature keeps emphasizing translational potential, but also highlights persistent limits around standardization, bioavailability, and clinical relevance. That makes a structured prioritization layer useful.

Useful references:

- translational logic for plant-derived bioactives in skin protection: https://www.mdpi.com/1467-3045/48/4/377
- plant-derived bioactive compounds in a broader One Health and translational framing: https://www.mdpi.com/2076-3417/16/1/327
- plant-derived bioactives and cancer-facing translational challenges: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40782283/

## Data classes to collect

For the first pass, prioritize:

- compound identifiers
- plant source
- bioactivity claim
- mechanism note
- target or pathway context
- evidence source
- translational limitations
- confidence level

## Required outputs

The first useful output should include:

- candidate shortlist table
- mechanism-linked ranking table
- translational risk/limitation notes
- one short report
- one to two explanatory figures

## Figure plan

The first visual package should include:

- shortlist ranking chart
- candidate-to-mechanism mapping figure
- translational opportunity vs limitation panel

## GitHub role

GitHub should hold:

- candidate schema
- ranking logic
- evidence and limitation notes
- figures
- report

## Hugging Face role

Hugging Face should hold:

- candidate shortlist explorer
- mechanism-linked ranking viewer
- translational plausibility interface

## Suggested first workflow

1. choose one bioactivity theme
2. pull candidates from `S2-B` and relevant `S2-A`/`S2-C` context
3. define translational evidence fields
4. rank the shortlist transparently
5. write the first translational report

## What not to do

Do not:

- present the shortlist as validated therapy
- hide weak evidence under marketing language
- collapse target, mechanism, and translational feasibility into one opaque score
