# S2-D-R1 Research Guide

## Title

Core Plant Defense Signature Panel

## Research home

- Sphere: `S2`
- Lane: `S2-D Plant-Pathogen & Defense Systems`
- Research line: `S2-D-R1`

## Research question

Which defense signatures are strong enough to serve as a compact, reusable plant-first panel for interpreting pathogen resistance across different plant systems?

## Why this line matters

`S2-D-R1` should not become a vague folder for "anything about pathogens".

It needs one stable center.

That center is a defense-signature panel made of:

- hormone-linked defense signals
- early immune responses
- defense-gene outputs
- defense-metabolite outputs

This gives `S2-D` a practical molecular identity before we move into more comparative host-pathogen interpretation.

## Scope

The first execution pass should stay narrow:

- bacterial and fungal resistance contexts
- plant-first signatures, not pathogen taxonomy
- a compact set of repeatable molecular and metabolite signals

## Main outputs

- defense signature evidence table
- ranked signature panel
- one summary figure of defense layers
- one shortlist of the most reusable markers for later studies

## Proposed substudies

### `S2-D-R1a` Hormone And Early-Response Signatures

Focus on:

- salicylic acid
- jasmonic acid / ethylene logic
- ROS burst
- callose deposition
- PR-gene induction

### `S2-D-R1b` Defense Metabolite And Pathway Signals

Focus on:

- camalexin and related phytoalexin logic
- phenylpropanoid-linked defense shifts
- flavonoid / phenolic defense accumulation

## Core evidence classes

- primary plant-pathogen studies
- transcriptome-metabolome integration studies
- mechanistic pathway studies

## What this is not

This is not yet:

- a crop deployment guide
- a pathogen control protocol
- a field intervention workflow

That belongs later in `S3` or in more translational layers.

## Immediate next step

Build the first compact panel with:

- 5-7 signature records
- clear evidence levels
- at least one bacterial and one fungal context
