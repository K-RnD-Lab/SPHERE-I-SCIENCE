# S4-B-R2 Report Skeleton

## Working title

From Pathway Perturbation To Mechanism Priority: A Confidence-Aware `S4-B` Framework

## Research home

- Sphere: `S4`
- Lane: `S4-B Pathway Chemistry & Perturbation`
- Research line: `S4-B-R2 Mechanism Prioritization`

## Question

Which pathway perturbation signals are strong enough to become mechanism priorities?

## Scope

Include:

- pathway-level perturbation evidence
- method sensitivity
- metabolite annotation confidence
- independent or cross-omics support

Exclude:

- unsupported pathway stories
- one-metabolite mechanism claims
- candidate interventions

## Methods

1. collect pathway perturbation records
2. normalize method and annotation fields
3. score support strength
4. separate mechanism priority from weak enrichment
5. draft process-level interpretation notes

## Outputs

- evidence table
- ranked mechanism-priority panel
- confidence vocabulary
- method-limit section

## Figures

- pathway-to-mechanism ranking table
- evidence-strength heatmap
- method-sensitivity comparison

## Limits

- metabolomics is not direct causality
- pathway databases are incomplete and uneven
- annotation uncertainty must be visible

## Conclusion

`S4-B-R2` should create a disciplined mechanism-prioritization layer, not a final causal answer.
