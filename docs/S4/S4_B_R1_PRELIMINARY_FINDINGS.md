- pathway ranking should reflect coherence and ambiguity, not p-values alone

## Current working logic

### 1. Pathway analysis in metabolomics is fragile if handled naively

The Nature Metabolism critique makes it clear that metabolite pathway analysis can become misleading when it inherits gene-set assumptions without metabolite-specific caution.

### 2. Perturbation maps are strongest when they are contrast-aware

Applied studies in organ injury, cancer, and cell models show that pathway comparison becomes useful when tied to explicit condition contrasts and metabolite support.

### 3. Integrative evidence improves mechanism value

When metabolomics is paired with transcriptomics or network logic, pathway interpretation becomes more defensible and better connected to later target reasoning.

### 4. Confidence language is part of the method

The best `S4-B` outputs should explicitly distinguish:

- coherent pathway disturbance
- partial support
- ambiguous or weak pathway inference

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- pathway perturbation maps can organize metabolomic findings into mechanism-facing structures
- pathway interpretation needs stronger discipline than standard enrichment habits
- confidence-aware ranking is central to usable pathway outputs

At this stage we should not say:

- that pathway ranking equals proof of causality
- that every enriched pathway is biologically meaningful
- that pathway maps alone resolve target prioritization
