# S4-D-R1 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Cross-Domain Signature Transfer: Shared Mechanism Motifs Across Disease, Model, Plant, And Environmental Metabolomics

## Research home

- Sphere: `S4`
- Lane: `S4-D Cross-Domain Mechanism Models`
- Research line: `S4-D-R1 Cross-Domain Signature Transfer`

## Question

Which recurring metabolomic and pathway patterns are transferable across domains strongly enough to support mechanism-level comparison?

## Scope

- human disease-state metabolomics
- clinical-vs-model comparison
- plant and environmental stress metabolomics
- shared vs distinct biochemical motifs
- transferability limits

## Data included

- large-scale human metabolome association work
- disease-family comparison studies
- clinical-animal comparison reviews
- plant stress metabolomics
- environment-linked plant toxic stress studies

## Methods

- extract recurring pathway families
- normalize dominant metabolite classes
- compare shared and context-specific motifs
- rank transferability with caution notes

## Outputs

- cross-domain motif evidence table
- transferable mechanism shortlist
- shared-vs-context-specific comparison note
- motif transfer report

## Figures

- domain-by-motif matrix
- shared vs distinct mechanism panel
- transferability confidence view

## Limits

- context-specific biology
- matrix and platform heterogeneity
- inconsistent pathway annotation
- overgeneralization risk across domains

## Conclusion

Cross-domain metabolomic transfer is useful when handled at the level of recurring mechanism motifs, not exact biomarker equivalence.
