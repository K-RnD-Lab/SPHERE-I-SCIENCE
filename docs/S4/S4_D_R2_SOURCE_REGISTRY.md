# S4-D-R2 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S4-D-R2 Shared Mechanism Interpretation`

It supports:

- recurring response-motif identification
- shared-vs-context-boundary logic
- transferability scoring across biological domains

## Current source set

### 1. Human metabolome-phenome atlas

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s42255-025-01371-1

Why it matters:

- large-scale human anchor for recurring metabolic patterns
- useful for distinguishing broad shared signals from disease-specific signals

### 2. Integrative metabolomics-genomics schizophrenia model

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-024-02568-8

Why it matters:

- model-system source connecting metabolomics to genetic and network context
- useful for shared mechanism inference beyond metabolite lists

### 3. Shared and distinct metabolomic alterations in AD and PSP

Source:

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39439201/

Why it matters:

- clear example of shared-vs-distinct motif logic in related neurodegenerative conditions
- useful for not overgeneralizing similar disease contexts

### 4. Organ-specific biochemical perturbations after colistin

Source:

- https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/molecular-biosciences/articles/10.3389/fmolb.2024.1338497/full

Why it matters:

- shows same exposure can create tissue-specific perturbation profiles
- useful as caution against universal mechanism transfer

### 5. Tobacco cold-stress metabolomics and transcriptomics

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0981942824001323

Why it matters:

- plant stress source for amino-acid, carbohydrate, and acclimation motifs
- useful for cross-domain stress motif comparison

### 6. Cumin under lead stress

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0048969724016383

Why it matters:

- environmental toxic-stress source
- useful for comparing oxidative and stress-adaptation motifs outside disease models

## Current stance

`S4-D-R2` should transfer motifs, not exact claims.

The safest unit of transfer is:

- motif
- process
- direction with caveat

The unsafe unit is:

- one exact biomarker treated as universal proof
