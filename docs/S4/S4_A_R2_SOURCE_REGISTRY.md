# S4-A-R2 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S4-A-R2 State-Specific Metabolomic Profiles`

It is meant to support:

- healthy-vs-perturbed profile logic
- subgroup and cohort comparison
- shared-vs-distinct metabolite interpretation
- caution around confounders and platform effects

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- state-specific metabolomic profiles
- group comparison and subgroup separation
- profile stability vs confounding variation
- examples where profiles are interpretable enough to carry into later pathway work

## Current source set

### 1. Healthy baseline profiles by age and sex

Source:

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/38491253/

Why it matters:

- strong baseline source for what healthy metabolomic variation already looks like
- prevents overclaiming disease-state specificity when age or sex already shifts the profile

### 2. Shared and distinct neurodegenerative metabolomic profiles

Source:

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39439201/

Why it matters:

- strong example of shared vs distinct state-profile interpretation
- useful for subgroup comparison logic rather than one-condition biomarker hunting

Source:

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S4-A-R2` is not trying to produce a universal metabolomic classifier.

It is trying to distinguish:

- strong state-profile signals
- subgroup differences worth carrying forward
- weak profile claims that may mostly reflect cohort structure or technical variation

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- papers with explicit healthy vs perturbed contrasts
- studies with more than one biological group
- sources that report confounders or platform differences clearly
- profile papers that can later bridge into pathway interpretation

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- metabolomic profiles can separate meaningful biological states
- subgroup comparison is often more useful than a one-biomarker mindset
- technical and cohort effects must be part of the interpretation

At this stage we should not say:

- that profile separation automatically proves mechanism
- that one cohort-specific profile generalizes universally
- that weakly matched platforms produce directly comparable state maps
