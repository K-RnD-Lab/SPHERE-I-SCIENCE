# S4-B-R1 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Pathway Perturbation Maps From Metabolomic Shifts: A Practical Evidence Review

## Research home

- Sphere: `S4`
- Lane: `S4-B Pathway Chemistry & Perturbation`
- Research line: `S4-B-R1 Pathway Perturbation Maps`

## Research question

How can metabolite-level shifts be translated into defensible pathway perturbation maps that highlight biochemical process change without misusing pathway analysis?

## Scope

This report covers:

- pathway perturbation ranking
- confidence-aware interpretation
- metabolite-to-pathway coherence
- comparison of pathway shifts across conditions

This report does not attempt:

- universal causal claims from enrichment outputs
- a full multi-omics integration framework
- a complete target-prioritization pipeline

## Data included

- methodology and critique papers
- metabolomics studies with pathway interpretation
- integrative metabolomics papers with mechanism-facing outputs

## Methods

1. collect evidence on pathway interpretation and misuse risks
2. normalize pathway labels, directions, and coherence signals
3. separate strong perturbation maps from enrichment noise
4. build a compact ranking and comparison structure

## Outputs

- one pathway evidence table
- one ranked disturbance panel
- one condition-to-pathway comparison map
- one concise report

## Figures

Possible figures:

1. pathway perturbation ranking panel
2. confidence vs ambiguity matrix
3. condition-to-pathway comparison diagram

## Limits

- metabolite annotation quality varies
- pathway boundaries are not always stable across databases
- some studies provide stronger biology than stronger methodology, and vice versa

## Conclusion

The report should conclude with:

- which pathway perturbations are most interpretable
- where pathway logic breaks down
- what should be carried forward into later mechanism or target analysis
