# S4-E-R2 Preliminary Findings

## Research home

- Sphere: `S4`
- Lane: `S4-E Translational Chemical Inference`
- Research line: `S4-E-R2 Evidence-Based Chemical Plausibility`

## Scope v0

This first execution pass narrows `S4-E-R2` to:

- candidate plausibility scoring
- evidence-layer comparison
- filtering of weak translational claims

## Current patterns

- candidate lists become useful only when evidence layers are explicit
- network pharmacology can inflate confidence when not critically constrained
- metabolomics supports discovery and prioritization, not direct validation alone
- natural-product profiling is valuable before target claims, not after overclaiming

## Practical interpretation

The useful output is a shortlist with evidence tiers:

- keep
- keep with caution
- hold for more evidence
- drop from translational claim

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- `S4-E-R2` can filter candidate plausibility
- stronger candidates need converging evidence
- method-risk flags are part of the product

At this stage we should not say:

- that plausibility means efficacy
- that a candidate is ready for intervention use
- that network pharmacology alone proves mechanism
