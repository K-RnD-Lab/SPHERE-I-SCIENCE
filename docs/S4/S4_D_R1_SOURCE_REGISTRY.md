# S4-D-R1 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S4-D-R1 Cross-Domain Signature Transfer`

It is meant to support:

- cross-domain mechanism comparison
- transferable response motifs
- shared-vs-context-specific biochemical logic
- caution against false universalism

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- recurring metabolomic motifs across different domains
- pathway families that reappear under distinct biological stresses
- comparison of disease, model, plant, and environmental stress settings
- mechanism transfer at motif level rather than exact biomarker level

## Current source set

### 1. Human metabolome-phenome atlas across diseases and traits

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s42255-025-01371-1

Why it matters:

- strongest large-scale source for identifying metabolite patterns shared across many human conditions
- useful as the human anchor for transferable motif logic

### 2. Shared and distinct metabolomic alterations in AD and PSP

Source:

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/39439201/

Why it matters:

- strong example of shared-vs-distinct pathway logic inside one disease family
- good source for how transfer should be handled within closely related contexts

### 3. Depression metabolomics across clinical and animal research

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0165032724000636

Why it matters:

- direct bridge between human and model-system metabolomics
- useful for identifying motif transfer across study types, not only diseases

### 4. Tobacco cold-stress metabolomics and transcriptomics

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0981942824001323

Why it matters:

- good plant-side source for amino-acid, carbohydrate, and stress-acclimation motifs
- useful for comparing generic stress logic with non-human systems

### 5. Cumin under lead stress

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0048969724016383

Why it matters:

- connects metabolomics to environmental toxic stress
- useful for cross-domain comparison of oxidative, transport, and stress-adaptation signatures

### 6. Pine under combined heat and drought stress

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0098847223000564

Why it matters:

- useful for recurring amino-acid, carbohydrate, flavonoid, and terpenoid response patterns
- good support source for transferable stress-metabolism motifs

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S4-D-R1` is not trying to prove that one domain fully predicts another.

It is trying to distinguish:

- recurring mechanism motifs worth transferring
- motifs that are only weakly shared
- patterns that are too context-bound to generalize

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- multi-condition human metabolomics
- animal-to-human comparison studies
- plant/environment stress metabolomics with clear pathway reporting
- papers that explicitly separate shared from distinct signals

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- some biochemical response motifs recur across very different biological contexts
- motif-level transfer is more defensible than exact biomarker transfer
- cross-domain comparison becomes useful when context limits are explicit

At this stage we should not say:

- that all stress systems are metabolically equivalent
- that one domain validates another automatically
- that recurrence alone proves mechanistic identity
