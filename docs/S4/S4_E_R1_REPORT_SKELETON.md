# S4-E-R1 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Candidate Chemical Prioritization From Metabolomic, Pathway, And Target Signals: A Practical Evidence Review

## Research home

- Sphere: `S4`
- Lane: `S4-E Translational Chemical Inference`
- Research line: `S4-E-R1 Candidate Chemical Prioritization`

## Research question

How can metabolomic, pathway, and target signals be translated into defensible candidate chemical shortlists for research prioritization?

## Scope

This report covers:

- candidate shortlist logic
- pathway-to-candidate bridging
- target-aware translational ranking
- ambiguity and confidence handling

This report does not attempt:

- direct intervention validation
- full medicinal chemistry optimization
- production-ready compound selection

## Data included

- translational metabolomics reviews
- compound and target shortlist papers
- critical methodological papers on network pharmacology and candidate inference

## Methods

1. collect evidence on candidate chemical inference from metabolic data
2. normalize pathway, target, and candidate support fields
3. separate stronger shortlist logic from weak speculative ranking
4. rank candidates by translational support and ambiguity

## Outputs

- one candidate evidence table
- one ranked candidate shortlist
- one pathway-to-candidate bridge panel
- one concise report

## Figures

Possible figures:

1. candidate-support ranking panel
2. pathway-target-candidate bridge map
3. translational confidence matrix

## Limits

- candidate ranking is only as good as the underlying pathway and target evidence
- many source papers still rely on weak network-pharmacology conventions
- chemical presence does not equal useful causal intervention

## Conclusion

The report should conclude with:

- which candidates are most defensible to prioritize
- where shortlist logic remains weak
- what should be carried into deeper mechanism or translational studies
