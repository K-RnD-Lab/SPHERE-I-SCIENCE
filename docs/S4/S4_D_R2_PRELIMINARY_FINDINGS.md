## Current patterns

- broad metabolic motifs can recur across disease and stress contexts
- related conditions can still have distinct profiles
- the same stressor can behave differently by tissue or organism
- plant and environmental stress data are useful for motif logic, not direct human transfer

## Practical interpretation

The strongest cross-domain output is a motif matrix:

- motif present
- motif direction
- context where it appears
- confidence tier
- boundary note

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- recurring motifs can guide cross-domain hypothesis generation
- motif-level transfer is more defensible than exact marker transfer
- context boundaries are part of the research product

At this stage we should not say:

- that all oxidative or stress signals are the same
- that one domain validates another
- that shared vocabulary equals shared biology
