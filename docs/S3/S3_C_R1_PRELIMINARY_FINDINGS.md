# S3-C-R1 Preliminary Findings

## Research home

- Sphere: `S3`
- Lane: `S3-C Crop Stress & Yield Systems`
- Research line: `S3-C-R1 Crop Stress And Support Profiles`

## Scope v0

This first active execution pass narrows `S3-C-R1` to:

- drought, heat, and salinity stress
- yield-relevant crop responses
- support factors that plausibly buffer stress outcomes

That scope is intentionally narrower than a full crop-resilience platform.

Without this narrowing, `S3-C-R1` would drift into a vague stress catalog with weak field interpretation value.

## Current stress and support shortlist

### Tier 1 stress signals

- reduced photosynthetic performance
- leaf wilting, yellowing, and canopy-level visual stress
- root-system impairment and water/nutrient uptake decline
- stage-specific yield sensitivity around flowering and grain filling

### Tier 1 support factors

- responsive root traits
- silicon-linked resilience support
- multi-trait monitoring rather than one-signal interpretation

### Tier 2 support factors

- crop-specific monitoring under drought-salinity combinations
- stage-aware stress assessment
- management strategies coupled to root and soil conditions

## Why these patterns matter

They jointly show three things:

- stress is not one undifferentiated condition
- yield loss depends on crop, growth stage, and stress combination
- support logic becomes stronger when it is tied to signals, not just treatments

## Practical interpretation

The current evidence already supports three real outputs:

1. a normalized crop-stress evidence table
2. a compact stress signal panel
3. a first support-factor ranking for field interpretation

## Current working logic

### 1. Signal interpretation must be multi-trait

The remote sensing and stress-detection literature both support that single-trait interpretation is often too weak under drought and salinity.

### 2. Root traits deserve high rank

The wheat abiotic-stress review supports root-system traits as one of the clearest support layers for stress resilience.

### 3. Growth stage matters for yield protection

The broader climate-resilience review supports that flowering and grain-filling stages are especially sensitive and therefore key for interpretation.

### 4. Silicon is a serious support-factor candidate

The silicon resilience paper supports keeping support-factor logic broader than only microbial interventions.

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- drought, heat, and salinity repeatedly threaten yield
- multi-trait signal interpretation is more defensible than single-indicator logic
- root traits and silicon are recurring support-factor candidates

At this stage we should not say:

- that one support factor works best for all crops
- that the current profile is enough for deployment-ready crop recommendations
- that remote sensing or signal detection alone solves the field problem

## Immediate next step

The next execution move for `S3-C-R1` should be:

- expand the evidence table with a few crop-specific field contexts
- keep only records that sharpen stress and support interpretation
- avoid bloating the table with generic stress summaries
