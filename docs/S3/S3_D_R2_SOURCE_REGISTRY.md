# S3-D-R2 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S3-D-R2 Intervention Portfolios, Scaling, And Context Transfer`

It is meant to support:

- portfolio design logic
- responsible scaling analysis
- context-transfer interpretation
- delivery and governance signals relevant to agricultural deployment

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- integrated intervention portfolios
- transfer across sites or organizational settings
- scaling friction and adaptation requirements
- combination logic that goes beyond one-input comparison

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S3-D-R2` is not trying to build a universal scaling framework.

It is trying to distinguish:

- portfolio combinations that look structurally stronger
- transfer conditions that require adaptation
- friction patterns that stop local success from scaling cleanly

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- comparative multi-site intervention studies
- field programs that document transfer failure or adaptation needs
- portfolio-like intervention bundles with clear success conditions
- sources that identify governance or advisory structures as scaling determinants

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- combination logic can be stronger than isolated intervention logic
- scaling is shaped by context, partnerships, and delivery structures
- responsible scaling requires adaptation rather than blind replication

At this stage we should not say:

- that any integrated portfolio automatically scales well
- that one country or program proves global transfer logic
- that biological efficacy alone predicts scaling success
