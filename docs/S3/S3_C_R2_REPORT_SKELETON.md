# S3-C-R2 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Recovery, Resilience, And Recurrent Stress Response In Crops: A Practical Evidence Review

## Research home

- Sphere: `S3`
- Lane: `S3-C Crop Stress & Yield Systems`
- Research line: `S3-C-R2 Recovery, Resilience, And Recurrent Stress Response`

## Research question

How can crop resilience be interpreted through recovery, recurrent-stress response, and yield stability instead of only through one-time stress indicators?

## Scope

This report covers:

- recovery after drought or combined stress
- priming and stress-memory logic
- recurrent-stress response
- yield-stability and reproductive resilience framing

This report does not attempt:

- a complete crop-breeding review
- a universal resilience metric
- a full agtech monitoring framework

## Data included

- direct recovery studies
- priming and stress-memory papers
- recurrent-stress and yield-stability evidence
- field vs controlled comparison sources

## Methods

1. collect source-backed evidence on recovery and recurrent stress
2. normalize resilience indicators and yield outcomes
3. separate recovery, resilience, and compensation logic
4. identify the signals most relevant for field interpretation

## Outputs

- one resilience evidence table
- one recovery and priming signal panel
- one yield-stability interpretation shortlist
- one compact report

## Figures

Possible figures:

1. resilience-state map
2. recovery vs yield-stability matrix
3. priming and recurrent-stress response diagram

## Limits

- many sources are crop- or genotype-specific
- priming evidence varies strongly by stage and context
- some studies are near-field or controlled rather than fully field-deployed

## Conclusion

The report should conclude with:

- what makes resilience more than simple survival
- which signals are most useful for recovery interpretation
- how recurrent stress changes the meaning of crop support logic
