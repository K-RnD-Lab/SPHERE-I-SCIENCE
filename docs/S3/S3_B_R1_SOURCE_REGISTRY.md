# S3-B-R1 Source Registry

## Research home

- Sphere: `S3`
- Lane: `S3-B Biofertilizer Design & Validation`
- Research line: `S3-B-R1 Biofertilizer Efficacy And Intervention Validation`

## Purpose

This file is the first real evidence layer for `S3-B-R1`.

It tracks:

- which sources we actually use
- which sources are conceptual versus field-validating
- what kinds of interventions are being compared
- which claims are strong enough to support a practical validation layer

## Current active scope

For the first execution pass, `S3-B-R1` is deliberately narrowed to:

- microbial biofertilizer efficacy under field-relevant conditions
- formulation and delivery logic
- intervention performance under real crop or stress contexts

This is a better first scope than trying to cover every microbial product category at once.

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S3-B-R1` should be treated as:

- a field-validation and intervention-ranking project

not as:

- a product recommendation engine
- a claim that all microbial inoculants work across contexts

## What to collect next

The next useful sources should include:

- crop-specific field trials under nutrient limitation
- carrier and shelf-life studies with deployment relevance
- reduced-fertilizer comparison studies
- consortium-vs-single-strain comparisons in additional crops

## Claim discipline

From this registry, we may claim:

- that some intervention patterns are repeatedly supported
- that formulation and field context strongly influence performance
- that consortia and co-application often outperform simplistic single-input logic

We should not yet claim:

- universal efficacy
- deployable product recommendations
- direct transferability across all crops and environments
