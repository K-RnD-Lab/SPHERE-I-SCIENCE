# S3-B-R2 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S3-B-R2 Biofertilizer Reliability, Quality Control, And Failure Modes`

It is meant to support:

- product trust assessment
- formulation reliability analysis
- failure-mode comparison
- future quality-checklist design

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- field inconsistency and product unreliability
- viability and labeling problems
- quality-control and regulatory gaps
- practical mitigation signals for deployment

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S3-B-R2` is not trying to prove that biofertilizers do not work.

It is trying to distinguish:

- plausible products
- low-trust products
- quality-control problems
- context-fit failures
- genuine design improvements

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- direct product-assessment studies
- formulation stability studies with measured shelf-life signals
- regulatory or standards-facing papers
- field papers that explicitly document failed or inconsistent performance

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- inconsistent performance is a structural issue, not a rare exception
- quality and viability metrics are central to deployment trust
- formulation, storage, and context fit are recurring reliability determinants

At this stage we should not say:

- that all commercial products are poor
- that one review resolves reliability standards
- that efficacy alone is enough to infer deployment readiness
