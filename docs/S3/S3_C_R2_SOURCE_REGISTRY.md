# S3-C-R2 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S3-C-R2 Recovery, Resilience, And Recurrent Stress Response`

It is meant to support:

- recovery profile interpretation
- priming and stress-memory logic
- recurrent stress comparison
- yield-stability framing under climate stress

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- post-stress recovery and rewatering
- stress memory or priming effects
- recurrent and combined stress response
- reproductive-stage and yield-stability consequences

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S3-C-R2` is not trying to define one universal resilience index.

It is trying to distinguish:

- rapid recovery
- useful resilience
- compensatory yield behavior
- priming-supported response
- reproductive-stage protection

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- field or near-field studies with explicit recovery metrics
- genotype comparisons with yield-stability outcomes
- repeated-stress studies that show when priming fails
- crop-specific resilience comparisons beyond wheat and barley

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- recovery and resilience are not identical to simple survival
- recurrent stress response depends on genotype and timing
- reproductive-stage protection and yield stability are central resilience outcomes

At this stage we should not say:

- that priming is broadly deployment-ready
- that one recovery metric captures agricultural resilience
- that greenhouse recovery always predicts field success
