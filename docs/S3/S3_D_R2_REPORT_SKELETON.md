# S3-D-R2 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Intervention Portfolios, Scaling, And Context Transfer In Agriculture: A Practical Evidence Review

## Research home

- Sphere: `S3`
- Lane: `S3-D Agro-Intervention Analytics`
- Research line: `S3-D-R2 Intervention Portfolios, Scaling, And Context Transfer`

## Research question

How can agricultural interventions be organized into context-aware portfolios that support responsible scaling and realistic transfer across sites and farming systems?

## Scope

This report covers:

- intervention portfolio combination logic
- scaling and context-transfer conditions
- adaptation requirements
- delivery and governance signals relevant to implementation

This report does not attempt:

- a universal scaling model
- a full agri-policy analysis
- a global economic ranking of interventions

## Data included

- integrated nutrient or intervention reviews
- scaling case studies
- responsible-scaling frameworks
- system-level combination studies

## Methods

1. collect evidence on combination and scaling logic
2. normalize portfolio types and transfer conditions
3. separate local-fit patterns from wider-scaling claims
4. identify repeated friction signals

## Outputs

- one portfolio evidence table
- one scaling-risk matrix
- one shortlist of stronger context-aware portfolio patterns
- one compact report

## Figures

Possible figures:

1. portfolio structure map
2. context-transfer risk matrix
3. scaling and adaptation loop diagram

## Limits

- evidence spans different farming systems and geographies
- some sources are framework-heavy rather than direct comparison studies
- organizational and governance signals may be harder to standardize

## Conclusion

The report should conclude with:

- which portfolio patterns look stronger
- where scaling tends to break
- what conditions justify adaptation instead of direct replication
