# S3-A To S3-E Research Map

## Purpose

This document turns `S3-A ... S3-E` into a practical lane-level map.

The goal is to keep:

- the lane stable
- two main research directions inside each lane
- one to two substudies inside each research direction

`S3` should stay intervention-facing and field-legible, not drift back into plant-intrinsic biology.

## Structural rule

Use:

- `S3-A`, `S3-B`, `S3-C`, `S3-D`, `S3-E` as stable lanes
- `R1`, `R2` as the two main directions inside each lane
- `R1a`, `R1b`, `R2a`, `R2b` as substudies when needed

## S3-A Soil & Rhizosphere Microbiomes

### Role

Soil-community interpretation, rhizosphere signals, and crop-support microbiome logic.

### `S3-A-R1` Rhizosphere Microbiome Scoring

#### Substudies

- `S3-A-R1a` support-marker discovery for crop-associated microbiomes
- `S3-A-R1b` microbiome risk and imbalance profiles

#### Why this stays central

This gives `S3` a clean biological backbone that still points toward applied agriculture.

### `S3-A-R2` Soil Microbiome State Classification

#### Substudies

- `S3-A-R2a` healthy vs stressed soil-community state logic
- `S3-A-R2b` crop-context microbiome comparison panels

#### Why this matters

This keeps microbiome work interpretable instead of turning into a vague catalog of taxa.

## S3-B Biofertilizer Design & Validation

### Role

Biological intervention design, validation logic, and evidence-aware formulation comparison.

### `S3-B-R1` Biofertilizer Efficacy And Intervention Validation

#### Substudies

- `S3-B-R1a` formulation comparison logic
- `S3-B-R1b` context-aware efficacy ranking

#### Why this stays central

This is the clearest practical line in `S3` and connects directly to field use.

### `S3-B-R2` Response Determinants And Failure Modes

#### Substudies

- `S3-B-R2a` variables linked to successful intervention response
- `S3-B-R2b` conditions associated with weak or failed outcomes

#### Why this matters

This prevents the sphere from making generic “biofertilizers are good” claims without validation logic.

## S3-C Crop Stress & Yield Systems

### Role

Field stress interpretation, crop support, and yield-relevant biological signal logic.

### `S3-C-R1` Crop Stress And Support Profiles

#### Substudies

- `S3-C-R1a` field stress signal panels
- `S3-C-R1b` support-factor ranking for crop recovery

#### Why this stays central

This keeps `S3-C` focused on practical biological interpretation rather than abstract productivity claims.

### `S3-C-R2` Yield-Relevant Biological Context

#### Substudies

- `S3-C-R2a` biological variables linked to better yield context
- `S3-C-R2b` stress-to-yield transition patterns

#### Why this matters

This gives `S3-C` a durable bridge between crop biology and practical agricultural relevance.

## S3-D Agro-Intervention Analytics

### Role

Decision logic for interventions, comparisons, and agricultural action scenarios.

### `S3-D-R1` Intervention Comparison Frameworks

#### Substudies

- `S3-D-R1a` compare biological intervention types
- `S3-D-R1b` context-based intervention recommendation logic

#### Why this stays central

This is the cleanest analytical layer for turning `S3` into a practical decision-support system.

### `S3-D-R2` Agro Scenario Scoring

#### Substudies

- `S3-D-R2a` stress and intervention scenario matrix
- `S3-D-R2b` field-priority ranking and flagging logic

#### Why this matters

This gives `S3-D` a strong translational identity without pretending to be a universal agronomy engine.

## S3-E Field Translation & Practical Deployment

### Role

Field-facing packaging of scientific logic into usable outputs for practitioners and programs.

### `S3-E-R1` Field Translation Playbooks

#### Substudies

- `S3-E-R1a` convert biological findings into field-readable notes
- `S3-E-R1b` deployment-ready interpretation templates

#### Why this stays central

This is where `S3` becomes legible to non-specialists without losing scientific grounding.

### `S3-E-R2` Practical Monitoring And Follow-Through

#### Substudies

- `S3-E-R2a` follow-up checkpoints for intervention monitoring
- `S3-E-R2b` practical signal dashboards and summaries

#### Why this matters

This keeps `S3-E` focused on implementation and observation rather than only reporting.

## Operational interpretation

### What is already useful now

Even before deep repo expansion, `S3` can become useful as:

- a rhizosphere interpretation layer
- a biofertilizer validation system
- an intervention comparison framework

### What should not happen

Do not let `S3` drift into:

- plant-only omics with no field logic
- phytochemical-first catalog work
- generic dashboards with no agricultural interpretation

Those belong in `S2` or `TECHNOLOGY`.

## Recommended order inside S3

Best order of execution:

1. `S3-A`
2. `S3-B`
3. `S3-D`
4. `S3-C`
5. `S3-E`

Reason:

- `S3-A` and `S3-B` define the scientific and applied core
- `S3-D` gives the first practical decision layer
- `S3-C` strengthens crop-context interpretation
- `S3-E` becomes stronger once the science and intervention logic are stable
