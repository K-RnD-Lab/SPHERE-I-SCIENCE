# S3-D-R2 Preliminary Findings

## Research home

- Sphere: `S3`
- Lane: `S3-D Agro-Intervention Analytics`

## Scope v0

This first active execution pass narrows `S3-D-R2` to:

- integrated intervention portfolios
- responsible scaling and transfer logic
- adaptation requirements across agricultural contexts

That scope is intentionally tighter than a broad innovation-systems discussion.

## Current portfolio and scaling shortlist

### Tier 1 patterns

- integrated nutrient portfolios rather than one-input systems
- portfolio performance that depends on crop mixture or layout
- scaling pathways that require partner alignment and local adaptation
- responsible scaling frameworks that explicitly model complexity

### Tier 2 patterns

- adaptive advisory systems around intervention portfolios
- context-aware digital support for portfolio deployment
- portfolio logic that combines biology, inputs, and advisory structure

## Why these patterns matter

They jointly show three things:

- intervention success does not automatically transfer across contexts
- portfolios are often stronger than isolated inputs, but harder to scale well
- governance, advisory structure, and local fit are part of intervention analytics

## Practical interpretation

The current evidence already supports three real outputs:

1. a normalized intervention-portfolio evidence table
2. a scaling and transfer-risk matrix
3. a first responsible-scaling interpretation note for agricultural portfolios

## Current working logic

### 1. Integrated portfolios can outperform isolated logic

The nutrient-management and intercropping sources support that combinations often produce stronger outcomes than single-mode inputs.

### 2. Scaling is not replication

The Ethiopia advisory and responsible-scaling sources show that local context, partner structure, and adaptation work shape whether an intervention moves beyond a pilot.

### 3. Transfer friction is predictable

Portfolio logic weakens when crop systems, social infrastructure, or delivery conditions change without adaptation.

### 4. Advisory and delivery layers belong inside intervention analytics

The context-aware systems source supports that implementation design cannot be separated from technical portfolio logic.

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- portfolio design and scaling logic belong inside `S3-D`
- stronger interventions often require stronger adaptation design
- responsible scaling is more defensible than naive scale-up claims

At this stage we should not say:

- that every portfolio should be made more complex
- that local-fit success proves wide transfer
- that the current pack is enough for production-ready scaling tools
