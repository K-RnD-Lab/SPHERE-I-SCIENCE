## Scope v0

This first active execution pass narrows `S3-E-R1` to:

- field translation of agricultural findings
- advisory structure and adoption logic
- practical playbook design

That scope is intentionally narrower than a full extension system analysis.

Without this narrowing, `S3-E-R1` would drift into generic communication language with weak practical value.

## Current translation shortlist

### Tier 1 patterns

- tailored advisory rather than generic broadcast guidance
- participatory or co-designed field learning
- explicit monitoring checkpoints in recommendations

### Tier 2 patterns

- barrier-aware guidance design
- advisory routes matched to target user
- follow-up logic after first deployment

## Why these patterns matter

They jointly show three things:

- translation is a design problem, not only a wording problem
- adoption depends on trust, fit, and practical follow-up
- the best playbooks tell people what to watch, not only what to do

## Practical interpretation

The current evidence already supports three real outputs:

1. a normalized field-translation evidence table
2. a compact playbook template
3. a first shortlist of adoption-supporting patterns

## Current working logic

### 1. Tailored advisory is stronger than generic messaging

The advisory and sustainability sources support that local adaptation and direct support usually outperform generic one-way information delivery.

### 2. Participation matters

The participatory agroecology source supports that co-created learning and field experimentation make translation more credible and usable.

### 3. Barriers are part of design

The extension barrier source supports that cost, trust, access, and relevance are not side issues; they are part of the playbook architecture.

### 4. Monitoring must be explicit

If a recommendation does not define what to monitor, it is much harder to judge whether it worked, failed, or needs adaptation.

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- field translation depends on advisory structure and local fit
- participatory and tailored approaches look stronger than generic outreach
- playbooks should include checkpoints, not only instructions

At this stage we should not say:

- that one advisory format works everywhere
- that adoption follows automatically from better wording
- that this current pack is enough for universal deployment design
