# S5-E-R1 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Longitudinal Brain Risk Panels: Blood, Aging-Clock, Imaging, And Cognitive Trajectory Signals

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-E Longitudinal Brain Risk Systems`
- Research line: `S5-E-R1 Longitudinal Brain Risk Panels`

## Question

Which longitudinal signals can be organized into practical brain-risk panels for future cognitive decline or dementia risk?

## Scope

- plasma proteomic risk markers
- brain and organ aging clocks
- AD blood biomarkers
- neurodegeneration markers
- cognitive trajectory modeling

## Data included

- longitudinal cohort studies
- systematic reviews and meta-analyses
- biomarker-panel studies
- trajectory-modeling reviews

## Methods

- extract follow-up horizon
- classify marker type
- map marker to endpoint
- record panel role and readiness level
- separate risk prediction from diagnosis

## Outputs

- longitudinal evidence table
- marker time-horizon panel
- trajectory class summary
- risk-readiness report

## Figures

- marker-by-time-horizon grid
- biomarker panel readiness ladder
- cognitive trajectory comparison map

## Limits

- cohort-specific transferability
- assay and platform differences
- endpoint heterogeneity
- clinical implementation gap

## Conclusion

Longitudinal brain-risk panels are strongest when marker type, time horizon, endpoint, and readiness level are recorded together.
