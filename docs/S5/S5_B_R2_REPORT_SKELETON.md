# S5-B-R2 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Contextual Vulnerability In Cognitive Aging: Risk, Reserve, And Modifiable Support Layers

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-B Cognitive Aging & Vulnerability`
- Research line: `S5-B-R2 Contextual Vulnerability Interpretation`

## Question

How can cognitive aging vulnerability be interpreted through layered risk and reserve context?

## Scope

- modifiable dementia risk factors
- cognitive reserve and support factors
- vascular and metabolic vulnerability
- social and engagement context
- risk-vs-resilience comparison

## Methods

- extract risk domain
- classify modifiability
- record cognitive endpoint
- separate vulnerability and resilience roles
- assign evidence strength

## Outputs

- contextual vulnerability evidence table
- risk-plus-reserve matrix
- modifiability readiness note
- interpretation report

## Figures

- life-course risk overlay
- vulnerability vs resilience grid
- modifiable factor readiness ladder

## Limits

- population risk does not equal individual prediction
- modifiability varies by setting
- social and lifestyle associations require careful causal framing

## Conclusion

Contextual vulnerability profiles are useful when risk factors, reserve, modifiability, and biological context are interpreted together.
