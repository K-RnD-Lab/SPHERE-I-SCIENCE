# S5-E-R1 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S5-E-R1 Longitudinal Brain Risk Panels`

It supports:

- time-aware brain-risk interpretation
- longitudinal biomarker panels
- cognitive trajectory comparison
- early-warning vs diagnostic claim discipline

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- future dementia or cognitive decline risk
- longitudinal marker evidence
- trajectory modeling
- readiness limits for practical use

## Current source set

### 1. Plasma proteomic prediction of future dementia

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s43587-023-00565-0

Why it matters:

- strong longitudinal cohort anchor
- identifies GFAP, NEFL, GDF15, and LTBP2 as important future dementia predictors

### 2. Organ-specific proteomic aging clocks

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s43587-025-01016-8

Why it matters:

- supports brain-specific proteomic aging as a risk-stratification layer
- useful for connecting organ-aging logic to dementia risk

### 3. Blood-based biomarkers and incident dementia

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s41591-025-03605-x

Why it matters:

- supports p-tau217 combined with NfL or GFAP for incident dementia prediction
- useful for practical biomarker-panel logic

### 4. Epigenetic clocks and brain health

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s41582-025-01105-7

Why it matters:

- useful longitudinal marker framing for minimally invasive brain-aging risk
- helps keep the panel broader than AD-only blood markers

### 5. NfL and longitudinal clinical decline in AD

Source:

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/41000329/

Why it matters:

- direct evidence synthesis for baseline NfL and later clinical decline
- useful as neurodegeneration progression marker

### 6. Group-based trajectory modeling for cognitive changes

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S1568163725002016

Why it matters:

- supports trajectory classes rather than single endpoint thinking
- useful for translating risk panels into longitudinal profiles

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S5-E-R1` is not trying to build a clinical screening tool.

It is trying to build:

- a risk-panel evidence structure
- a time-horizon classification
- a trajectory-aware interpretation layer

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- longitudinal cohorts with clear follow-up
- studies reporting incident dementia or cognitive decline
- biomarker-panel comparisons
- trajectory models with biological correlates

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- some blood, imaging, and aging-clock markers support future-risk stratification
- time horizon and endpoint definition are central to interpretation
- longitudinal panels are stronger than one-marker claims

At this stage we should not say:

- that these markers are ready for broad public self-diagnosis
- that incident-risk prediction equals clinical diagnosis
- that one cohort model transfers universally
