# S5-E-R2 Research Guide

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-E Longitudinal Brain Risk Systems`
- Research line: `S5-E-R2 Predictive Risk And Progression Signals`

## Why this study belongs here

`S5-E-R2` extends longitudinal panels into predictive interpretation. It focuses on early-warning signals, progression markers, and time-based stratification without presenting research biomarkers as consumer diagnosis.

## Core question

Which longitudinal markers and model types are useful for separating early risk, progression monitoring, and clinical-stage support?

## Practical substudies

### `S5-E-R2a` Early-Warning Interpretation Layers

Goal:

- classify signals that may appear before diagnosis or measurable decline

Focus:

- blood proteins
- brain aging clocks
- p-tau, GFAP, NfL
- trajectory features

### `S5-E-R2b` Time-Based Risk Stratification Logic

Goal:

- define how time horizon changes interpretation

Focus:

- short-term vs long-term prediction
- incident diagnosis vs decline rate
- monitoring vs screening vs trial enrichment

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- longitudinal markers can support risk stratification and progression interpretation
- time horizon is central to marker meaning
- panels are stronger than one-marker prediction

At this stage we should not say:

- that predictive-risk markers are standalone clinical screening tools
- that biomarker positivity equals future diagnosis
- that a model trained in one cohort transfers automatically
