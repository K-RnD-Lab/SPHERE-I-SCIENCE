# S5-A-R2 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Neuroinflammatory Biomarker Specificity: Matrix, Stage, And Panel-Role Interpretation

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-A Neuroinflammation & Biomarkers`
- Research line: `S5-A-R2 Biomarker Context And Specificity`

## Question

How should neuroinflammatory and glial biomarkers be interpreted across matrix, disease stage, and specificity limits?

## Scope

- GFAP, YKL-40, sTREM2, cytokines, NfL, and related markers
- AD and related dementia contexts
- CSF vs plasma interpretation
- single-marker vs panel logic
- standardization limits

## Data included

- systematic literature reviews
- biomarker standardization roadmap
- blood biomarker meta-analysis
- extracellular vesicle biomarker meta-analysis
- imaging/fluid complementarity review

## Methods

- extract biomarker and biological role
- classify matrix and disease stage
- record specificity issue
- classify panel role
- record claim limits

## Outputs

- biomarker specificity evidence table
- marker-role matrix
- stage and matrix comparison note
- panel-readiness report

## Figures

- biomarker matrix map
- specificity vs panel-role grid
- CNS inference caution ladder

## Limits

- nonspecific inflammatory signals
- matrix and assay differences
- stage heterogeneity
- limited causal interpretation

## Conclusion

Neuroinflammatory biomarkers are most useful when treated as context-specific panel components, not standalone disease definitions.
