# S5-D-R1 Research Guide

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-D Brain Resilience & Recovery`
- Research line: `S5-D-R1 Resilience Marker Profiles`

## Why this is the right next `S5` study

`S5-D-R1` prevents `S5` from becoming only a decline and disease sphere. It adds protective biology, cognitive reserve, brain maintenance, and resilience interpretation.

## Core question

Which biological, cognitive, imaging, and reserve-related signals help explain preserved cognitive function despite aging, pathology, stress, or risk burden?

## Practical substudies

### `S5-D-R1a` Protective Biomarker Or Signal Panels

Goal:

- identify markers and profile features associated with preserved cognition or resistance to decline

Focus:

- cognitive reserve
- brain maintenance
- structural and functional imaging patterns
- biofluid and molecular resilience markers

### `S5-D-R1b` Support-Factor Ranking

Goal:

- rank candidate support factors that may contribute to resilience

Focus:

- education and reserve proxies
- lifestyle and psychosocial factors
- multimodal biomarker context
- intervention-readiness limits

## Why this matters now

Brain-aging research increasingly recognizes that pathology burden does not map cleanly to cognitive function. Some people maintain cognition despite biomarkers, lesions, or aging-related changes. `S5-D` is the lane that keeps that protective pattern visible.

## Data classes to collect

For the first useful pass, prioritize:

- cognitive reserve indicators
- education and occupational complexity proxies
- imaging markers
- biofluid or molecular markers
- pathology burden
- cognitive outcome or preservation measure

## Required outputs

The first useful output should include:

- resilience evidence table
- support-factor ranking panel
- pathology-vs-performance interpretation note
- short resilience profile report

## GitHub role

GitHub should hold:

- resilience schema
- evidence table
- reserve and support-factor notes
- figures
- report

## Hugging Face role

Hugging Face should hold:

- resilience profile explorer
- reserve-vs-pathology comparison view
- support-factor ranking dashboard

## Suggested first workflow

1. separate reserve proxies from biological markers
2. extract pathology burden and cognitive outcome
3. record whether the source supports resilience, resistance, or recovery
4. rank evidence strength cautiously
5. write a short protective-profile interpretation

## Evidence fields to standardize

At minimum, track:

- `resilience_domain`
- `marker_or_factor`
- `pathology_or_stressor`
- `protected_endpoint`
- `profile_role`
- `evidence_source`
- `evidence_level`
- `interpretation_note`
- `limit_note`

## What not to do

Do not:

- claim that reserve eliminates disease risk
- confuse association with causal protection
- treat education or lifestyle proxies as direct biological mechanisms
- frame resilience as personal responsibility rather than a research construct
