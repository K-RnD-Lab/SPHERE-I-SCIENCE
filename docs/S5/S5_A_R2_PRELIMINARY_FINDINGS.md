## Scope v0

This first active execution pass narrows `S5-A-R2` to:

- neuroinflammatory marker specificity
- matrix and disease-stage context
- panel-supportive vs standalone biomarker roles

## Current specificity shortlist

### Tier 1 patterns

- GFAP is one of the strongest glial activation markers in AD-related biomarker discussions
- YKL-40, sTREM2, MCP-1, and related inflammatory markers may be useful but often lack disease specificity
- inflammatory biomarkers are stronger in panels than as standalone disease-defining signals

### Tier 2 patterns

- blood inflammatory changes should not be treated as direct proof of CNS inflammation
- extracellular vesicle biomarkers are promising but require careful interpretation
- imaging and fluid biomarkers are complementary rather than mutually exclusive

## Why these patterns matter

They jointly show three things:

- `S5-A` needs specificity discipline, not only marker collection
- matrix and stage context are essential
- panel logic is scientifically stronger than single-marker overclaim

## Practical interpretation

The current evidence already supports three real outputs:

## Current working logic

### 1. Neuroinflammatory markers are context-sensitive

The same marker can mean different things depending on disease stage, matrix, assay, and comorbidity.

### 2. Single-marker stories are weak

The standardization roadmap explicitly supports panel-based interpretation and warns against simple causal inference from association studies.

### 3. Complementarity is the right design principle

Fluid, imaging, and clinical markers should be combined by role rather than treated as competing replacements.

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- neuroinflammatory biomarkers can support AD and ADRD research panels
- specificity and matrix context determine usefulness
- panel role classification is necessary before practical application

At this stage we should not say:

- that GFAP or any inflammatory marker alone diagnoses AD
- that peripheral inflammation proves brain inflammation
- that exploratory EV or digital markers are deployment-ready
