# S5-C-R1 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S5-C-R1 Neurodegeneration State Models`

It supports:

- disease-state model construction
- biomarker-stage comparison
- cross-condition neurodegeneration interpretation
- diagnostic vs prognostic role separation

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- biomarker-based state and stage models
- AD and Lewy body disease as first anchors
- protein aggregate imaging and fluid biomarkers
- digital biomarker caution

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S5-C-R1` is not trying to build a clinical diagnostic model.

It is trying to build:

- a disease-state evidence schema
- biomarker role definitions
- stage-readiness interpretation

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- cross-disease biomarker comparisons
- Parkinson, Lewy body, ALS, or FTD state models
- assay-readiness and cut-point papers
- studies that separate pathology detection from clinical stage

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- biomarker-defined neurodegeneration states are becoming central to research interpretation
- AD has the most mature staging framework
- non-AD proteinopathy markers are increasingly important

At this stage we should not say:

- that every biomarker is clinically interchangeable
- that digital biomarkers are already validated as diagnostic replacements
- that AD staging logic fully transfers to all neurodegenerative diseases
