## Scope v0

This execution pass focuses on:

- layered cognitive vulnerability
- modifiable risk domains
- reserve and resilience context

## Current patterns

- risk interpretation is strongest when vascular, social, sensory, lifestyle, and reserve signals are not separated artificially
- cognitive reserve can change the meaning of pathology and imaging markers
- social isolation and loneliness should be treated as distinct but overlapping vulnerability dimensions
- technology use and cognitive engagement may act as protective or reserve-adjacent signals, but causality remains difficult

## Practical interpretation

The first useful output is a vulnerability overlay:

- risk domains
- modifiability
- reserve or resilience modifiers
- cognitive endpoint
- evidence strength

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- contextual profiles can make cognitive aging risk more interpretable
- modifiable risks and support factors should be tracked together
- reserve and vulnerability interact

At this stage we should not say:

- that one lifestyle factor prevents dementia
- that social or technology exposure is universally protective
- that population risk factors are enough for individual prediction
