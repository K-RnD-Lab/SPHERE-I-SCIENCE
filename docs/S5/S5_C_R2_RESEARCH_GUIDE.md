# S5-C-R2 Research Guide

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-C Neurodegeneration Models`
- Research line: `S5-C-R2 Progression And Pattern Distinction`

## Why this study belongs here

`S5-C-R2` extends disease-state models into progression and cross-condition distinction. It asks which biomarkers help separate AD, PD, FTD, MSA, ALS, and other neurodegenerative patterns without pretending one marker explains all diseases.

## Core question

How can progression-associated signals and disease-specific patterns be compared across neurodegenerative conditions?

## Practical substudies

### `S5-C-R2a` Progression-Associated Signal Tracking

Goal:

- identify markers linked to severity, decline, or progression

Focus:

- NfL, GFAP, tau, p-tau, alpha-synuclein
- imaging progression
- longitudinal change

### `S5-C-R2b` Compare Degeneration Profiles Across Conditions

Goal:

- separate shared and disease-specific neurodegeneration patterns

Focus:

- AD vs PD vs FTD
- atypical parkinsonism
- proteinopathy and pathway overlap

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- shared and disease-specific patterns can be compared across neurodegenerative conditions
- NfL is useful for progression but limited in specificity
- proteinopathy and plasma proteomic layers can improve disease distinction

At this stage we should not say:

- that one marker cleanly separates all neurodegenerative diseases
- that progression markers are always diagnostic
- that cross-disease overlap means identical mechanism
