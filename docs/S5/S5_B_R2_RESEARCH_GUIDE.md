# S5-B-R2 Research Guide

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-B Cognitive Aging & Vulnerability`
- Research line: `S5-B-R2 Contextual Vulnerability Interpretation`

## Why this study belongs here

`S5-B-R2` extends cognitive aging beyond marker lists. It asks how age, reserve, vascular health, social connection, lifestyle, and modifiable risks interact when interpreting cognitive vulnerability.

## Core question

How can cognitive aging vulnerability be interpreted through layered context rather than isolated risk factors?

## Practical substudies

### `S5-B-R2a` Age-Plus-Risk-Factor Vulnerability Overlays

Goal:

- combine age, vascular, metabolic, social, sensory, and lifestyle risks into interpretable overlays

Focus:

- modifiable dementia risk factors
- vascular and metabolic context
- sleep, hearing, social isolation, depression, air pollution, and injury context

### `S5-B-R2b` Resilience-Vs-Risk Comparison Profiles

Goal:

- compare vulnerability profiles with resilience and reserve signals

Focus:

- reserve proxies
- technology or cognitive engagement
- social connection
- modifiable support factors

## Data classes to collect

- risk domain
- risk factor or protective factor
- age or life-course window
- cognitive endpoint
- modifiability note
- resilience or reserve note
- evidence strength

## Required outputs

- contextual vulnerability evidence table
- risk-plus-reserve profile grid
- modifiability and readiness note
- short interpretation report

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- cognitive vulnerability is layered and context-dependent
- modifiable risk factors and reserve signals can inform profile interpretation
- risk and resilience should be compared together

At this stage we should not say:

- that every risk factor has equal evidence or equal modifiability
- that lifestyle framing replaces biological markers
- that population risk translates directly to individual prediction
