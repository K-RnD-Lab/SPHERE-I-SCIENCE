# S5-B-R1 Report Skeleton

## Working title

Cognitive Aging Risk Profiles: A Multidomain Vulnerability Framework

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`
- Lane: `S5-B Cognitive Aging & Vulnerability`
- Research line: `S5-B-R1 Cognitive Aging Risk Profiles`

## Question

Which multidomain signals can be organized into practical vulnerability profiles for cognitive aging?

## Scope

- cognitive-domain change
- vascular and metabolic risk
- biological aging markers
- reserve and resilience factors
- modifiable and nonmodifiable risk layers

## Data included

- reviews of cognitive aging mechanisms
- brain-age and epigenetic-aging evidence
- modifiable risk-factor reviews
- vascular/endothelial risk evidence

## Methods

- extract risk domains
- separate marker type from profile role
- classify risk vs protective signal
- record interpretation limits

## Outputs

- vulnerability evidence table
- profile category map
- cognitive-domain comparison panel
- short risk interpretation report

## Figures

- multidomain vulnerability map
- risk vs resilience profile grid
- marker-readiness ladder

## Limits

- association evidence is not intervention proof
- risk factors vary by population
- biomarker availability and interpretation differ by setting

## Conclusion

Cognitive aging risk profiles are most useful when they integrate cognition, biology, vascular context, reserve, and modifiable factors without reducing aging to one marker.
