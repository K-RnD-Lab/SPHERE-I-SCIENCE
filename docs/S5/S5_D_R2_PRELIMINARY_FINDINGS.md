## Scope v0

This execution pass focuses on:

- recovery after stroke, TBI, and acquired brain injury
- reserve vs recovery separation
- biomarker and imaging interpretation

## Current patterns

- cognitive reserve may influence rehabilitation and clinical recovery after stroke or TBI
- chronic TBI involves inflammatory, glial, and neurodegeneration biomarker patterns
- post-stroke cognitive recovery is influenced by intervention type, vascular context, and longer-term dementia risk
- imaging and network markers can support recovery interpretation but need cautious prognostic framing

## Practical interpretation

The first useful output is a recovery matrix:

- pre-event reserve
- acute or chronic injury signal
- recovery phase
- intervention or rehabilitation context
- cognitive or functional endpoint

## Current discipline

At this stage we may say:

- recovery can be structured as a research profile
- reserve and rehabilitation response are related but not identical
- biomarkers can support monitoring and prognosis research

At this stage we should not say:

- that one biomarker predicts recovery for an individual
- that all brain injuries share one recovery biology
- that intervention evidence is uniform across stroke and TBI
