## Working title

Predictive Brain-Risk Signals: Early Warning, Progression Monitoring, And Time-Based Stratification

## Research home

- Sphere: `S5`

## Question

How should longitudinal biomarkers and models be interpreted across early risk, progression monitoring, and disease-stage support?

## Scope

- future dementia risk
- blood biomarker combinations
- brain and organ aging clocks
- NfL progression evidence
- computational progression models

## Methods

- classify time horizon
- separate marker role
- record endpoint
- score readiness
- flag transferability limits

## Outputs

- predictive-risk evidence table
- marker-role matrix
- time-horizon interpretation panel
- readiness report

## Figures

- time horizon vs marker role matrix
- early-warning vs progression grid
- readiness ladder

## Limits

- cohort transferability
- endpoint heterogeneity
- assay and model differences
- implementation gap

## Conclusion

Predictive brain-risk signals are useful only when time horizon, endpoint, marker role, and readiness level are kept explicit.
