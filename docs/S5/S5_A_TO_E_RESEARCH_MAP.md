# S5-A To S5-E Research Map

## Purpose

This document turns `S5-A ... S5-E` into a practical lane-level map.

The goal is to keep:

- the lane stable
- two main research directions inside each lane
- one to two substudies inside each research direction

`S5` should stay brain- and aging-focused, not become a generic health sphere.

## Structural rule

Use:

- `S5-A`, `S5-B`, `S5-C`, `S5-D`, `S5-E` as stable lanes
- `R1`, `R2` as the two main directions inside each lane
- `R1a`, `R1b`, `R2a`, `R2b` as substudies when needed

### `S5-A-R1` Neuroinflammation Signal Panels

#### Substudies

- `S5-A-R1a` ranked neuroinflammatory marker panels
- `S5-A-R1b` immune-context interpretation profiles

This is one of the clearest translational entry points in `S5`.

## Operational interpretation

### What is already useful now

Even before deep repo expansion, `S5` can become useful as:

- a neuroinflammation interpretation layer
- a cognitive aging vulnerability system
- a longitudinal brain-risk framing structure

### What should not happen

Do not let `S5` drift into:

- generic wellness language
- all-purpose health analytics
- disease naming with no biomarker or risk logic

Those weaken the scientific identity of the sphere.

## Recommended order inside S5

Best order of execution:

1. `S5-A`
2. `S5-B`
3. `S5-E`
4. `S5-C`
5. `S5-D`

Reason:

- `S5-A` and `S5-B` create the strongest translational entry point
- `S5-E` adds long-horizon structure
- `S5-C` becomes stronger once risk and biomarker logic are already in place
- `S5-D` benefits from the surrounding framework
