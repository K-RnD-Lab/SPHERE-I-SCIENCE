# S5-A-R2 Source Registry

## Purpose

This file records the first evidence layer for:

- `S5-A-R2 Biomarker Context And Specificity`

It supports:

- neuroinflammatory biomarker interpretation
- specificity and overlap analysis
- CSF vs blood matrix comparison
- panel-role classification

## Active scope

Current scope is deliberately narrow:

- neuroinflammatory and glial biomarkers
- AD and related dementia contexts
- specificity and standardization limits
- complementary use with imaging and core biomarkers

## Current source set

### 1. Neuroinflammatory fluid biomarkers in AD

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s41380-025-02939-9

Why it matters:

- systematic literature review across AD stages and long-term outcomes
- strong source for GFAP, YKL-40, sTREM2, and cytokine context

### 2. Roadmap for inflammatory fluid marker interpretation

Source:

- https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12974-025-03432-4

Why it matters:

- strongest methods and standardization source
- directly warns against single-marker and blood-only overinterpretation

### 3. Tracking neuroinflammatory biomarkers in AD

Source:

- https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s12974-024-03163-y

Why it matters:

- useful for longitudinal and ATI(N)-style biomarker framing
- highlights GFAP and other candidates in stage context

### 4. Blood biomarkers of neurodegeneration and glial activation in AD

Source:

- https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S053155652500289X

Why it matters:

- systematic review and meta-analysis of GFAP, NfL, YKL-40, and related markers

### 5. Extracellular vesicle biomarkers in ADRD

Source:

- https://www.nature.com/articles/s44400-024-00002-y

Why it matters:

### 6. Imaging and fluid biomarkers as complementary tools

Source:

- https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/40480828/

## Current research stance

At this stage, `S5-A-R2` is not trying to certify a clinical biomarker panel.

It is trying to build:

- a marker specificity table
- a panel role taxonomy
- a matrix and stage interpretation layer

## What to collect next

Next source additions should prioritize:

- AD and ADRD biomarker studies with stage labels
- CSF and plasma comparisons
- longitudinal marker analyses
- papers reporting specificity limits clearly

## Claim discipline

At this stage we may say:

- neuroinflammatory markers are useful as panel components
- GFAP, YKL-40, sTREM2, and related markers have stage and context relevance
- standardization and matrix interpretation are central to usefulness

At this stage we should not say:

- that a single inflammatory marker defines disease state
- that blood inflammatory changes directly prove CNS inflammation
- that nonspecific biomarker elevation is enough for diagnosis
